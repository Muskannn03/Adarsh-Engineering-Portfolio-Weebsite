About Dr. Arpit Sohane

Super-Specialist in Neonatal Intensive Care

Dr. Arpit Sohane is a DM-trained Neonatologist with comprehensive experience in managing critical newborns in Level III Neonatal Intensive Care Units (NICU). Equipped with advanced training in neonatal ventilation and point-of-care ultrasound (POCUS), he brings cutting-edge clinical skills to complex newborn care and high-risk pediatric follow-ups.

With active involvement in clinical research, quality-improvement (QI) initiatives, and neonatal emergency transports, his primary objective is to improve survival rates and long-term neurodevelopmental outcomes of extremely preterm and critical infants.

Medical Services

Clinical Expertise & Services

Providing advanced clinical care in intensive units and specialized outpatient follow-up.

Level III NICU Super-Specialist Care

Comprehensive management of critical neonates in high-capacity, advanced Level III Neonatal Intensive Care Units.

  • Preterm & LBW Care: Special focus on extremely preterm (less than 28 weeks) and low birth weight infants.
  • Neurocritical Care: Head-cooling therapies (Therapeutic Hypothermia), Near-infrared Spectroscopy (NIRS), and amplitude-integrated EEG (aEEG).
  • iNO Therapy: Inhaled Nitric Oxide administration for persistent pulmonary hypertension (PPHN).
  • Advanced Procedures: Umbilical line insertions, PICC line placement, arterial line placement, exchange transfusion, and Total Parenteral Nutrition (TPN) management.

Point-of-Care Ultrasound (POCUS)

Utilizing high-end ultrasound scans at the bedside for rapid diagnostics, targeted therapies, and hemodynamic management.

  • Neonatal Lung POCUS: Bedside monitoring of respiratory distress syndrome (RDS), transient tachypnea (TTN), and prediction of extubation success.
  • Neuro-Ultrasound (Brain): Monitoring intraventricular hemorrhage (IVH) and periventricular leukomalacia (PVL) in preterms.
  • Abdomen & Line Placements: Guiding line insertions and screening for Necrotizing Enterocolitis (NEC).
  • Functional Echocardiography: Real-time assessment of PDA (patent ductus arteriosus), neonatal shock, and pulmonary hypertension.

Advanced Neonatal Ventilation

Expertise in managing complex respiratory conditions in preterms using state-of-the-art ventilation techniques.

  • Non-Invasive Ventilation: High-flow nasal cannula (HFNC), CPAP, and Non-Invasive Positive Pressure Ventilation (NIPPV).
  • Invasive Ventilation: Conventional pressure/volume-targeted modes tailored for neonates.
  • High-Frequency Ventilation: High-Frequency Oscillatory Ventilation (HFOV) for severe respiratory failures.
  • Advanced NRP: Certified Advanced Neonatal Resuscitation Program (NRP) trainer and clinician.
